The easiest polynomial to solve by radicals is one of the form \(x^n - a\text{.}\) As we discussed in Chapter 4, the roots of \(x^n - 1\) are called the nth roots of unity. These roots are a finite subgroup of the splitting field of \(x^n -1\text{.}\) By Corollary 22.1.11, the \(n\)th roots of unity form a cyclic group. Any generator of this group is called a primitive nth root of unity.
