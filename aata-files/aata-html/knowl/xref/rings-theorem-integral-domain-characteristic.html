Theorem 16.2.8.

The characteristic of an integral domain is either prime or zero.

Proof.

Let \(D\) be an integral domain and suppose that the characteristic of \(D\) is \(n\) with \(n \neq 0\text{.}\) If \(n\) is not prime, then \(n = ab\text{,}\) where \(1 \lt a \lt n\) and \(1 \lt b \lt n\text{.}\) By LemmaÂ 16.2.7, we need only consider the case \(n 1 = 0\text{.}\) Since \(0 = n 1 = (ab)1 = (a1)(b1)\) and there are no zero divisors in \(D\text{,}\) either \(a1 =0\) or \(b1=0\text{.}\) Hence, the characteristic of \(D\) must be less than \(n\text{,}\) which is a contradiction. Therefore, \(n\) must be prime.
