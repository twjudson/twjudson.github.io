Example 15.2.8.

Let \(G\) be a finite group of order \(p^n\text{,}\) \(n \gt 1\) and \(p\) prime. By Theorem 14.2.4, \(G\) has a nontrivial center. Since the center of any group \(G\) is a normal subgroup, \(G\) cannot be a simple group. Therefore, groups of orders \(4\text{,}\) \(8\text{,}\) \(9\text{,}\) \(16\text{,}\) \(25\text{,}\) \(27\text{,}\) \(32\text{,}\) \(49\text{,}\) \(64\text{,}\) and \(81\) are not simple. In fact, the groups of order \(4\text{,}\) \(9\text{,}\) \(25\text{,}\) and \(49\) are abelian by Corollary 14.2.5.
