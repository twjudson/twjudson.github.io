Exercise 20. Cyclotomic Polynomials.

The polynomial
\begin{equation*} \Phi_p(x) = \frac{x^p - 1}{x - 1} = x^{p - 1} + x^{p - 2} + \cdots + x + 1 \end{equation*}
for \(p\) prime is called the cyclotomic polynomial. Show that \(\Phi_p(x)\) is irreducible over \({\mathbb Q}\) for any prime \(p\text{.}\)
Hint.
The polynomial
\begin{equation*} \Phi_n(x) = \frac{x^n - 1}{x - 1} = x^{n - 1} + x^{n - 2} + \cdots + x + 1 \end{equation*}
is called the cyclotomic polynomial. Show that \(\Phi_p(x)\) is irreducible over \({\mathbb Q}\) for any prime \(p\text{.}\)
